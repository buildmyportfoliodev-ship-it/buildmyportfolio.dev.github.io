Investigated the mysterious death of Sir Charles Baskerville on the Dartmoor moors. Uncovered an elaborate murder plot disguised as a supernatural curse, exposing the villain's use of a phosphorescent-coated hound to terrorise the Baskerville family and secure a vast inheritance. Murder Investigation

A Study in Scarlet

Solved a complex double murder at Lauriston Gardens, tracing the crime to a revenge plot spanning two continents. This was the first case formally documented by Dr. John Watson, showcasing the power of logical deduction in criminal investigations and establishing my reputation with Scotland Yard as an indispensable consultant.

Deductive Reasoning Observation Profiling
Forensic Research

Tobacco Ash Classification System

Developed a comprehensive classification system for 140 varieties of pipe, cigar, and cigarette tobacco. This pioneering forensic work enables investigators to identify tobacco residue at crime scenes, narrowing the suspect pool by smoking habits. Now widely adopted by law enforcement agencies as a standard forensic reference tool.

Chemistry Classification Forensic Science
Academic Publication

Upon the Distinction Between the Ashes of Various Tobaccos

Published a landmark monograph detailing the chemical properties and visual characteristics of tobacco ashes from over a hundred different origins. This research pioneered forensic chemistry in criminal investigations and remains a foundational text referenced by modern forensic scientists globally. Presented to the Royal Society of Chemistry in 1883.

Forensic Chemistry Publication Methodology
Criminal Investigation

The Sign of Four

Unravelled the mystery of the Agra treasure and the disappearance of Mary Morstan's father, exposing a four-man conspiracy spanning two decades. The investigation demanded tracking skills, cipher-breaking, and a dramatic river pursuit down the Thames — demonstrating the full breadth of investigative and physical capabilities required in the field.

Tracking Cryptography Investigation
